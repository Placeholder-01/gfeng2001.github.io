About Me

I’m currently at UC Berkeley studying EECS. Since I was little, I’ve been passionate about solving problems, from getting lego robots with missing pieces to work as a kid to using machine learning to help with lung cancer diagnosis over the past few summers.

My personal philosophy is that I should go to sleep every night a better person than the me that woke up in the morning. Thus, I strive to improve myself every day, as a student, as an engineer, and as a member of my community.

Community

On top of developing myself intellectually, I've also emphasized the growth of my community. This is reflected in many of my activities.
I was a recipient of The Leadership Award from the Cal Alumni Association in August 2019.
  • In Spring 2018, I helped compile and solve problem sets at AlphaStar Academy for use in the contest prep class curriculum.
  • In Fall 2017, I taught a math contest (AMC 10) class at Silicon Valley Youth, a nonprofit organization that hosts classes for local students in the community and donates all revenue to local school districts. I developed the AMC 10 class curriculum, which comprised of writing lecture handouts and creating worksheets by compiling problems from past contests and also creating new problems.
  • From 2015 to 2019, I was a tutor at Homestead MathLab, where I helped other students with math homework and test preparation. I tutored subjects ranging up to Calculus and Statistics.
  • On top of captaining Science Bowl at my high school, I created an outreach program with the Science Bowl team at Sunnyvale Middle School (which I had founded in my 8th grade year). I helped coach Sunnyvale Science Bowl from a brand new club into a regional powerhouse, with top placements in 2017 and 2018.

I'm also passionate about health and fitness, which I see as a physical manifestation of my personal improvement philosophy. In my free time, my hobbies include lifting weights, shooting hoops, following basketball and football, and playing Go (Weiqi).

Work Experience

  • From 2017 to 2019, I was a Computational Biology research intern at Snyder Lab in Stanford's School of Medicine. My mentor was Hayan Lee.
    • 2017 - Using Python, I applied statistical models to analyze methylation entropy of the 23 chromosome pairs of sample patients at different stages of carcinoma.
    • 2018-2019 - I cleaned and processed data sets of Lung Adenocarcinoma patients, and applied RandomForest machine learning to develop a model that makes predictions on how many days a patient has left based on given conditions. The research that I completed contributed to a paper I co-authored with my mentor, which was submitted to the 2020 Pacific Symposium on Biocomputing.
  • I am very experienced with problem solving and algorithms. In January 2018, I qualified for the USA Computing Olympiad (USACO) Gold Division. I am also a five-time American Invitational Mathematics Examination (AIME) qualifier.
